How Does a Gambling Habit Differ Neurobiologically from a Chemical Abuse Addiction?

Addiction is a condition characterized by compulsive drug seeking and use, despite harmful consequences. Gambling addiction is a problem that is often overlooked. The signs and symptoms of gambling addiction are very similar to those of other addictions. However, there are some key distinctions between gambling addiction and chemical abuse addiction.

The neurological mechanisms underlying gambling addiction are not well understood. However, research suggests that there are similarities between gambling addiction and chemical abuse addiction. Both types of addiction involve alterations in brain chemistry and circuitry.

Addictive drugs such as cocaine and methamphetamine cause the release of dopamine in the brain. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that plays a role in reward-motivated behavior. Addictive drugs increase dopamine levels in the brain, which results in feelings of pleasure and euphoria.

Gambling also causes the release of dopamine in the brain. This may explain why people with gambling addiction become addicted to gambling. Gambling may provide the same kind of rush as addictive drugs.

However, there are also important distinctions between gambling addiction and chemical abuse addiction. Gambling addiction is not associated with physical dependence or withdrawal symptoms. In contrast, people who are addicted to drugs often experience withdrawal symptoms when they stop using the drug.

People with gambling addiction also tend to have higher rates of co-occurring psychiatric disorders than people with chemical abuse addictions. People with gambling addictions are more likely to suffer from anxiety disorders, mood disorders, and eating disorders than people with chemical abuse addictions.

Gambling addiction is a serious problem that should be treated by a professional therapist or counselor. If you or someone you know has a problem with gambling, please seek help immediately.

Is Gambling a Form of Drug Abuse?
